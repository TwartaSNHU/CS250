Module Eight Journal

Question #1
How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?

For me, interpreting user needs starts with really understanding what the user is trying to accomplish—not just what they say they want. Breaking those needs into user stories helps a lot because it forces me to think in a simple format: who the user is, what they want to do, and why it matters. That “why” is the most important part. It keeps development focused on delivering value instead of just building features. User stories also make it easier to turn ideas into actual tasks, test cases, and priorities, which helps bridge the gap between planning and coding.

Question #2
How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

When I approach developing programs, I try to keep things iterative instead of trying to build everything at once. I like the idea of starting small, getting something working, and then improving it over time. Agile fits well with that mindset. In the future, I’d want to incorporate things like sprint planning, maintaining a backlog, and regular check-ins (like daily stand-ups) to stay on track. I also think retrospectives are important because they give you a chance to step back and improve how you work, not just what you build. Overall, I want my process to stay flexible so I can adapt as requirements change.

Question #3
What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
To me, being a good team member comes down to communication, reliability, and being open to feedback. You have to be able to clearly share your ideas, ask questions when something isn’t clear, and keep others updated on your progress. At the same time, it’s important to be dependable and follow through on your work so the team can rely on you. I also think being open-minded is key—everyone brings different perspectives, and being willing to listen and adjust leads to better results overall. A strong team isn’t just about technical skill, it’s about how well people work together.
